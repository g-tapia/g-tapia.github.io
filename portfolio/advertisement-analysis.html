Instructor: Vijay Gurbani (Chief Data Scientist at Vail Systems, Inc)

Data Analysis Summary

  • Performed selective data extraction and analysis on TV, radio, and newspaper advertising data.
  • Generated scatter plots to visually analyze the relationship between sales and each advertising medium.
  • Calculated correlation coefficients to quantify the impact of each advertising medium on sales.
  • Conducted a budget expenditure analysis to identify patterns in advertising spending.

About the Dataset

This dataset, titled "Advertising.csv," contains data designed for linear regression analysis. It includes 200 records and 5 features: TV, Radio, Newspaper, and Sales, which serve as the independent variables, with Sales being the target variable. The dataset is useful for exploring relationships between advertising spending and sales outcomes, making it ideal for predictive modeling and business analytics.

Libraries Used: dplyr, psych

Key Findings

The analysis revealed that TV advertising has the most significant positive impact on sales, followed by radio and newspaper. The scatter plots and correlation coefficients provided a clear visual and quantitative representation of these relationships. Additionally, the budget expenditure analysis suggested an optimal distribution strategy for maximizing sales.

Data Visualizations

Scatter plots for each advertising medium were created to visualize their impact on sales. These plots highlighted the relationships and trends in the data, aiding in the analysis.

Conclusions

The project successfully demonstrates the ability to handle, analyze, and interpret complex datasets, providing valuable insights into the effectiveness of different advertising strategies.

Conclusion

From the analysis, it is evident that the most effective advertising strategy involves prioritizing TV advertising, as it consistently correlates with higher sales. Although a balanced budget across different media types can yield results, placing more emphasis on TV advertising is a more efficient approach for maximizing returns. As such, companies should consider allocating a significant portion of their advertising budget to TV to optimize sales performance, while also ensuring some investment in radio and newspaper for a well-rounded approach.
